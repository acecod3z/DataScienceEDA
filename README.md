# DataScienceEDA
Project Description

Business Context

The United Nations Global Terrorism Analysis (UNGTA) dataset provides a comprehensive, open-source repository of terrorist incidents from 1970 to 2017. This extensive collection is meticulously curated to offer valuable insights into terrorist activities across the globe. With over 180,000 recorded attacks, the UNGTA serves as a critical tool for understanding the evolving landscape of terrorism.

The dataset is managed by a collaborative team of experts under the auspices of the United Nations, focusing on providing accurate and detailed information about terrorist events worldwide. The data includes a wide range of attributes, such as the date, location, and type of attack, as well as information about the perpetrators, targets, and outcomes.

Key features of the UNGTA dataset include:

1. Event Specifics:

Detailed records of each terrorist attack, including date, location, and the nature of the incident (e.g., assassination, bombing, hostage-taking).

2. Geographic and Demographic Information:

Insights into the geographic regions affected, including country, region, and city specifics, as well as details about targeted groups or individuals.

3. Attack Details:

Information on the attack methods used, weapons involved, and the nature of the attack (e.g., explosive, incendiary, or armed assault).

4. Impact Assessment:

Data on casualties, including the number of killed or wounded, and any property damage or ransom demands associated with the attacks.

Main Libraries to be used:

Pandas for data manipulation, aggregation
Matplotlib and Seaborn for visualisation and behaviour with respect to the target variable. Use at least 5 different visualisations.
NumPy for computationally efficient operations
